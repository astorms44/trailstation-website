Trailstation — Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 4, 2026
Last updated: June 4, 2026
Publisher: Austin Storms
Contact: stormsunity@gmail.com

This is the privacy policy for Trailstation, an iPhone and Apple Watch app for building and playing time-synced audio “stations” from audio you import.

We wrote this in plain English. If anything is unclear, write to the contact address above.

TL;DR

  • Trailstation collects no data.
  • It makes no network connections — it works entirely offline.
  • No accounts, no analytics, no ads, no tracking, no third-party SDKs.
  • The audio you import and the stations you build stay on your device. We never see them.

What we collect

Nothing. Trailstation has no servers and makes no network connections. There is no analytics, no advertising, no account, and no login. We, the developer, never receive any information about you or your use of the app.

What the app stores on your device

Everything the app needs lives in its own private storage on your device (its app sandbox). This includes:

  • Audio files you import. When you build a station, you choose audio files through Apple’s standard file picker. Copies of those files are stored in the app’s private storage so it can play them offline.
  • Stations you build. The station name, the order of tracks, and the crossfade setting.
  • Small app settings. Minor bookkeeping the app uses to keep your iPhone and Apple Watch in step (for example, which stations it has already sent to your watch). This is stored on-device using the system’s standard preferences storage and is never transmitted to us.

This data is created by you, for you, and stays on your device. None of it is sent to the developer or to any third party.

Sharing a station

Trailstation lets you share a station as a single self-contained .trailstation file using the iOS share sheet — for example over AirDrop, Messages, or Mail. When you do this, you are sending your own file to a recipient you choose. The file contains the audio and station details you put into it. Trailstation has no part in this beyond packaging the file at your request; it does not upload the file anywhere, and the developer never sees it. Whatever service you choose to send it through (e.g. Messages) is governed by that service’s own privacy policy.

Apple Watch

If you send your stations to a paired Apple Watch, the audio is transferred directly between your own iPhone and your own Apple Watch using Apple’s on-device Watch Connectivity framework. It does not pass through any server, and the developer never sees it.

What we do not collect

  • We do not collect your name, email, phone number, address, or any contact information.
  • We do not collect your location.
  • We do not collect device identifiers, advertising identifiers, or analytics data.
  • We do not collect usage patterns, listening history, or which stations you build.
  • We do not run any third-party analytics SDK (no Firebase, no Mixpanel, no Sentry, no Crashlytics).
  • We do not run any advertising SDK.
  • We do not make network requests of any kind.

What we share with third parties

Nothing. Because the app collects no data and makes no network connections, there is nothing to share. There is no analytics provider, no advertising network, no data broker, and no server-side vendor.

How we protect your data

  • By design: because your data never leaves your device, there is no server, no database, no logs, no backups, and no employees with access to it.
  • At rest: iOS and watchOS encrypt on-device app data when your device is locked.

Your rights and how to exercise them

You are in full control at all times.

  • Delete a station: swipe to delete it inside the app. This also removes the audio files that station used from the app’s storage. You can do the same on the Apple Watch.
  • Delete everything: delete the Trailstation app from each device. iOS and watchOS remove all of the app’s on-device data when the app is deleted.

If you live in a jurisdiction with statutory data-subject rights (EU/UK GDPR, California CCPA/CPRA, etc.), you have the right to request a copy of any data we hold about you and to request its erasure. Because Trailstation collects no data and we never receive any, our answer is: we hold no data about you.

Children

Trailstation collects no data from anyone, including children. The audio that plays in the app is audio you choose to import yourself.

Changes to this policy

If we change this policy in any meaningful way, we will update the “Last updated” date above and note the change in the Changelog below. We will never quietly start collecting data.

Contact

Questions, requests, or concerns: stormsunity@gmail.com

We try to reply within a few business days. We are a small team — please be patient.
